About Me

The start of my career in game design and development started when I was recruited to fill in as Interim Lead Designer for Grayabyss LLC. At that time, I was fresh off New Jersey Institute of Technology with some project management skills that they needed to lead a small team remotely for a game app.

After graduating with a M.S. Game Design and Development degree from Rochester Institute of Technology, I have found myself working as an instructor for Game-U where I work with students across the USA teaching them how they can bring their game ideas into reality. Outside of work, I venture new game prototypes which leads to small indie games and explore new skills including 3D modeling. I have even published curriculum in the company of the recent game engine: Godot.

Since I grew up in the New Jersey town of West Orange, the home of Thomas Edison, I am influenced by one of his quotes:

"I never did a day's work in my life. It was all fun."

There is still much I am willing to learn, and my work in this industry has been the most enjoyable experience I ever have. I welcome any opportunity that comes my way to be a growing part of it.
